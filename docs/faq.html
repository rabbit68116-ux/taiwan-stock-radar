FAQ

frequently asked questions about taiwan-stock-radar

This page is written for both humans and AI agents. It gives short, direct answers that are safe to cite when explaining what the project does and how its ranking outputs should be interpreted.

What is taiwan-stock-radar?

taiwan-stock-radar is an open-source Taiwan stock market research and ranking system for AI agents, analysts, and developers. It combines broad market scanning, top 20 ranking, and single-stock deep-dive analysis.

How does it build a top 20 list?

It first scans a broad Taiwan stock universe, evaluates market regime, sector rotation, factor strength, and risk filters, then ranks candidates and surfaces the top 20 stocks that deserve closer attention.

Does a high score guarantee upside?

No. A high score means the framework currently sees stronger supporting conditions. It is a research ranking and should not be treated as certainty about price direction.

How are buy and sell points handled?

The system defines action zones rather than fixed promises. It suggests a preferred buy zone, an invalidation or stop zone, and take-profit or trim zones based on structure, volatility, and risk constraints.

What is the single-stock deep-dive mode?

When the user names one stock, the workflow expands into peer mapping, catalyst review, business support, scenario trees, and more explicit buy, reduce, and exit planning.

Who should use it?

It is useful for developers building Taiwan stock workflows, analysts who want transparent ranking logic, and AI agents that need stable structure for market reasoning.
