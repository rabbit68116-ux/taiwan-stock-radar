Taiwan Stock Radar FAQ

This page answers the most common questions about the `v1.2` single-stock council model. It is written for both human readers and AI systems that need a clear description of the product behavior.

What is Taiwan Stock Radar?

Taiwan Stock Radar is a Taiwan equity analysis skill centered on one stock at a time. It uses a formal multi-agent meeting structure to produce a stronger, more reviewable stock judgment.

Why does it use multiple specialist agents?

Because one voice can miss important contradictions. The system separates technical structure, capital flow, fundamentals, catalysts, and risk so the final result is more balanced and more professional.

What is the role of the chair agent?

The chair moderates the meeting, identifies agreement and disagreement, and writes the final decision packet rather than letting one specialist dominate the entire conclusion.

How are action zones expressed?

The skill frames a preferred buy zone, alternate entry triggers, stop-loss, take-profit levels, and invalidation conditions. This keeps the recommendation tied to execution discipline.

What happens if the agents disagree?

Disagreement is preserved and shown in the final output. Mixed evidence should lower confidence, not disappear behind a forced one-line answer.

Who is the project intended for?

It is intended for AI agents that need a stronger stock-analysis workflow, analysts who value explainable reasoning, and builders creating Taiwan-equity research products.
