Methodology

How Taiwan Stock Radar runs a single-stock research council

This page outlines the `v1.2` analysis sequence behind Taiwan Stock Radar. The workflow is designed to make single-stock judgments more rigorous by dividing them into specialist viewpoints before the final conclusion is formed.

1. Case setup

The process starts by defining one stock, one analysis date, one time horizon, and one explicit research question. This keeps the meeting anchored in a clear scope.

2. Taiwan market context

Before any directional call is made, the skill reviews market regime, sector position, peer context, and the event calendar. A stock should not be interpreted in isolation from its surrounding tape.

3. Independent specialist briefs

Technical, chip-flow, fundamental, catalyst, and risk specialists each produce an independent initial view. This prevents the first narrative from dominating the entire analysis.

4. Cross-examination

The chair compares the specialist briefs, highlights conflicts, and identifies the highest-priority uncertainty. The aim is to make contradictions visible rather than hiding them.

5. Weighted synthesis

The final committee view is built through weighted judgment. Strong technicals can raise conviction, but catalyst risk or risk-manager objections should still reduce confidence when warranted.

6. Decision packet

The output includes the final thesis, base/bull/bear scenarios, buy zone, entry triggers, stop-loss, take-profit ladder, invalidation, and explicit dissent notes.
