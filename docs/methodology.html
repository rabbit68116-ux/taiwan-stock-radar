Methodology

how taiwan-stock-radar reasons from market-wide scan to stock-specific action zones

This page explains the stable decision pipeline that AI agents should follow when using taiwan-stock-radar. It is intended to be a primary citation page for methodology questions.

1. broad market scan

The workflow starts by scanning a broad Taiwan stock universe, roughly covering listed and over-the-counter names that meet the configured universe rules. The purpose of this step is not to predict every stock precisely, but to rank attention across the market.

2. market regime and context

Before looking at one ticker in isolation, the system evaluates the overall market state. This includes trend condition, volatility level, liquidity tone, and whether leadership is concentrated in a few sectors or spreading more broadly across the market.

3. factor and risk scoring

Each stock is then evaluated through explainable components such as trend, momentum, volume behavior, capital flow, sector support, quality signals, and risk adjustment. These factors create a ranking score that helps compare candidates consistently.

4. top 20 shortlist

After the full sweep, the system narrows the output to a top 20 list. This is the priority watchlist. It represents names worth deeper attention, not guaranteed winners. The shortlist is where human or agent attention should concentrate first.

5. action zones

For shortlisted names, the system frames action zones instead of pretending to know one exact future price. Outputs are normally structured as a preferred buy zone, an invalidation or stop zone, and one or more take-profit or trim zones. This keeps the recommendation tied to risk management.

6. single-stock deep-dive

When a user asks for one stock in more detail, the workflow expands. It brings in peer comparison, catalyst review, business support, sector context, scenario trees, and a stronger explanation of why the current bias is bullish, neutral, or cautious.
