Methodology

How Taiwan Stock Radar moves from market surveillance to stock-level action framing

This page outlines the analytical sequence behind Taiwan Stock Radar. It is intended to show how the skill interprets Taiwan equities in a disciplined order, rather than treating every stock prompt as an isolated question.

1. Market-wide surveillance

The workflow starts with a broad Taiwan stock universe. The purpose of this stage is not to form a prediction on every name, but to build situational awareness and establish where attention should go first.

2. Regime and context assessment

Before interpreting any one stock, the skill evaluates broader conditions such as trend character, volatility tone, liquidity environment, and sector leadership concentration. This ensures stock analysis is anchored in market context.

3. Factor and risk interpretation

Each candidate is then examined through explainable components such as trend, momentum, volume behavior, capital flow, sector support, quality signals, and risk adjustment. These components create a more disciplined ranking process.

4. Top 20 research shortlist

After the broader scan, the system narrows its focus into a Top 20 shortlist. This is not a promise of future performance. It is a professional research filter designed to highlight where deeper work is most justified.

5. Action-zone framing

For shortlisted names, the skill defines a preferred buy area, an invalidation or stop area, and a take-profit or trim framework. This converts ranking output into a more execution-aware research result.

6. Single-stock deep-dive expansion

When the user requests one specific stock, the workflow expands into a memo-style structure that includes thesis, peers, catalysts, sector context, scenario framing, and a more explicit action plan.
