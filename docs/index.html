Project Statement

Taiwan Stock Radar demonstrates what a serious stock-analysis skill should look like. It is designed to help an AI system move from market-wide observation to a ranked research agenda, and from there to a more disciplined single-stock view.

Market coverage with intent

The skill begins with the wider Taiwan stock universe rather than isolated ticker-level prompts, allowing the agent to develop a coherent market view before making stock-specific judgments.

Shortlist before conviction

Instead of pretending every stock deserves the same depth of analysis, the framework narrows attention into a Top 20 shortlist that reflects current opportunity quality.

Single-stock analysis with structure

When a specific ticker is requested, the system expands into a memo-style output that includes thesis, context, action zones, and explicit risk framing.

Research outputs that can be challenged

The goal is not to sound certain. The goal is to produce work that can be reviewed, compared, improved, and defended.

Exhibit summary: Taiwan Stock Radar is a professional Taiwan equity analysis skill that shows how an AI agent can scan a broad market universe, rank the most relevant opportunities, and translate that ranking into a structured research output with buy, stop, and take-profit framing.

Why It Matters

In most stock-related AI demos, the model speaks in broad sentiment. Taiwan Stock Radar is designed to show a more rigorous alternative: a workflow that prioritizes market context, ranking discipline, and execution-aware interpretation.

From vague opinion to ranked judgment

The skill helps the agent explain why one stock deserves attention before another, instead of producing interchangeable commentary.

From prediction to planning

It reframes stock analysis around zones, scenarios, and invalidation logic, which is more useful than presenting one exact price view as certainty.

From demo to reusable framework

The project is presented as a skill that can evolve into dashboards, scans, backtests, and more robust market research tools.

Core Workflow

The workflow is deliberately simple to describe and disciplined enough to reuse. That is what allows an AI agent to behave less like a generic chatbot and more like a research assistant with method.

1 Scan and normalize Taiwan stock data
2 Assess regime, factors, and risk
3 Produce a ranked Top 20 shortlist
4 Frame action zones and invalidation logic
5 Expand into a deep-dive stock memo

Single-Stock Deep-Dive Example

A serious analysis skill should move beyond opinion and into a more structured memo. The example below shows the kind of professional framing Taiwan Stock Radar aims to support.

Research View

  • Stock: 2330 台積電
  • Core thesis: market leadership, earnings quality, and stronger institutional support.
  • Context: best interpreted within a stable or constructive market regime.
  • Catalyst lens: earnings cycle, sector rotation, and AI supply-chain sentiment.
  • Risk lens: event gaps, macro volatility, and sector re-rating pressure.

Action Plan

  • Preferred buy zone: pullback support or breakout confirmation with controlled risk.
  • Invalidation: a structural break that weakens the original setup thesis.
  • Take-profit framework: scale decisions around expansion rather than one exact target.
  • Confidence: medium-high only while market regime and sector support remain aligned.
  • Follow-up task: compare with peers and revisit catalyst timing before raising conviction.
