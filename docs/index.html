Project Statement

Taiwan Stock Radar demonstrates how an AI stock skill can become more rigorous by splitting one difficult judgment into several specialist perspectives. The goal is not louder certainty. The goal is better process, clearer disagreement, and more useful action planning.

One stock, not a noisy watchlist

The `v1.2` product is explicitly centered on a chosen TWSE or TPEX name. It treats single-stock judgment as the main event rather than as a leftover note after a screen.

Multiple specialist AI analysts

Technical, chip-flow, fundamental, catalyst, and risk perspectives are separated first, then synthesized by a chair agent into a final research verdict.

Visible disagreement

The system is designed to preserve dissent when evidence is mixed. A professional output should show what could go wrong, not just what sounds attractive.

Decision-oriented output

The final packet includes scenario framing, buy zones, stop-loss logic, take-profit ladders, and thesis-break conditions.

Specialist Agent Council

The system uses a weighted research council so that no single analytical style controls the entire conclusion.

Meeting Workflow

The council is structured so that each specialist can think clearly before the final conclusion is formed.

1 Case setup: stock, date, horizon, question
2 Independent specialist briefs
3 Cross-examination and conflict review
4 Weighted consensus and dissent logging
5 Final decision packet with action plan

Representative Output

A serious stock skill should deliver more than a directional opinion. It should produce work that can be reviewed, challenged, and acted on with discipline.

Research packet

  • Stock context: market, sector, peer map, and current regime.
  • Specialist notes: separate technical, chip-flow, fundamental, catalyst, and risk briefs.
  • Scenario tree: base case, bull case, and bear case with trigger conditions.
  • Consensus map: what the council agrees on and what remains contested.

Action plan

  • Preferred buy zone: where risk-reward becomes acceptable.
  • Entry triggers: aggressive entry vs conservative confirmation.
  • Risk control: stop-loss, invalidation, and event-risk caveats.
  • Exit framework: TP1, TP2, and trim logic tied to scenario quality.

Why This Skill Matters

For AI agents

It provides a reusable structure for handling stock questions with less improvisation and more defensible reasoning.

For analysts

It demonstrates how single-stock work can be split into roles, challenged, and reassembled into a clearer decision memo.

For builders

It turns the analysis method itself into a portable skill with configs, blueprints, and a public-facing showcase.

Risk note: Taiwan Stock Radar is a research and decision-support skill. It does not promise investment results and should not be interpreted as certainty about future price behavior.
