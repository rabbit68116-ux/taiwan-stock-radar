How Taiwan Stock Radar is applied in practice

Taiwan Stock Radar is designed to support more than one style of work. Depending on the question, it can behave as a market surveillance layer, a ranking engine, or a stock-specific research assistant.

Market surveillance mode

Used when the question is broad, such as what deserves attention across Taiwan equities today. The skill scans the wider universe, interprets regime and sector context, and produces a ranked shortlist.

Shortlist refinement mode

Used when a smaller candidate set already exists. The skill compares names more carefully and explains which ones should move up or down in research priority.

Single-stock memo mode

Used when the user asks for one specific stock. The skill expands beyond ranking and produces a more structured memo with thesis, context, action zones, and risk framing.

Review mode

Used when revisiting a previous setup or recommendation. The skill can compare the original framing with later market behavior and assess whether the analysis logic remained coherent.
